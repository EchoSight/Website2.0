Bat Surveys: Essential Information for Clients

What Is a Bat Survey?

A bat survey is a professional ecological assessment, carried out to detect the presence of bats and their roosts on your site. Whether you’re planning construction, development, or renovation, a survey is often a legal requirement if bats could be present. Using state-of-the-art thermal imaging, acoustic analysis, and direct observation, we deliver reliable, defensible results—every time.

Why Are Bat Surveys Important?

Bats and their roosts are legally protected in the UK. Failing to identify their presence can result in legal action, fines, costly project delays, and reputational harm. A thorough, compliant bat survey protects your project, your investment, and the local environment.

What’s Involved in a Bat Survey?

  • Preliminary Assessment: Desktop study and habitat review to identify potential for bats.
  • On-site Survey: Advanced equipment deployed by licensed experts—night-time thermal and acoustic monitoring, targeted observations.
  • Analysis & Reporting: All data is expertly analysed, producing clear, actionable recommendations in a report that stands up to scrutiny.

Who Needs a Bat Survey?

  • Developers and construction professionals
  • Architects and planning consultants
  • Homeowners near woodlands, water, or older buildings
  • Land managers and local authorities

If your project could disturb bats or their habitats, a survey is not just recommended—it’s essential.

Legal Protection & Legislation

Bats are protected by UK and European law, including:

  • Wildlife & Countryside Act 1981
  • Conservation of Habitats and Species Regulations 2017

It is a criminal offence to:

  • Deliberately capture, harm, or kill a bat
  • Disturb bats or damage/destroy a roost—even accidentally

Surveys and mitigation measures must be carried out by a licensed professional.

Risks of Skipping a Survey

Neglecting proper bat surveys can lead to:

  • Refused planning applications
  • Legal action and prosecution
  • Financial penalties and costly project delays
